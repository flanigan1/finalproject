Pretty Lights, Burning Lights

A new piece of fiction by a new author

Fiction by Brady Flanigan

Everyone was pressed up against each other and the railing---three feet from the edge of the stage where the lights were glowing out over the six-thousand people and into the dark. People were stacked on each other's shoulders and crawling over concrete barriers, falling, stumbling over tufts of grass on the sloped lawn.

And in the front---one body from the front was Two, watching the lights glow over the shoulder of the blond in the black corduroy dress in front of him. She was up against the railing with her huge ass pressed against his waist. She wasn't event watching the music. Her eyes were locked onto the edge of the concrete stage, while she twisted around to the sound of the LSD music in her head...the music Two imagined she was dancing to, but he knew she wasn't. She was too composed. And too beautiful. He was pushed behind her when a guy in a lab coat and swim trunks threw-up Budweiser and ran off into the dark. The whole crowd had to shuffle aside to make room for it.

He thought she looked just like his mother. He never got to see her face though. She never turned around to look at him---only grinded into his waist, and little by little, made Two certain he was in love with the faceless blond with the huge-moving-ass in the black dress. He stood there and listened to the music blur and distort, as the voices behind him wrapped around his head.
